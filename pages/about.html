whoami

Cybersecurity guy from Australia focused on red teaming and offensive security. I've always leaned more toward the human side — social engineering, physical security, understanding where people's trust breaks down — but I spend plenty of time in terminals too, breaking boxes on HackTheBox and TryHackMe and building tooling to make the process better.

I also picked up lockpicking along the way, because security doesn't stop at the keyboard.

Background

Came up through a few desk jobs before committing to cybersecurity full-time. Finished my B.S. in Information Technology (Cybersecurity & Networking) and earned my CompTIA Security+ in 2024. Currently finishing a Master of Cybersecurity and working toward the AWS Security Specialty cert.

The goal is to contribute to Australia's cybersecurity space in a meaningful way — always looking for opportunities where I can keep learning and actually make an impact.

What I'm Into

  • Social Engineering — The human side of security, from phishing to physical intrusion
  • Red Teaming — Full-scope adversary simulation
  • AI + Security — Fine-tuning LLMs for offensive security workflows
  • Lockpicking — Physical security is still security
  • Tool Development — Building what I need when it doesn't exist

Current Project

Fine-tuning a Qwen3 14B model for cybersecurity operations. It runs locally on an RTX 3090 via Ollama, connected to 12 MCP tool servers for things like nmap, sqlmap, hashcat, and radare2. I benchmark it against live HTB boxes, log every decision the model makes, and feed the results back into training. The write-ups on this site come out of that process.
